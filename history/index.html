History of combined sewer overflows

The Metropolitan Water Reclamation District of Greater Chicago tracks whenever they release raw sewage in to Chicago area rivers. Since January 1st 2007, raw, untreated sewage has been dumped in to rivers on 660 days.

These occurrences are referred to as combined sewer overflow events, or CSOs.

Note: This site is no longer active and does not reflect the most recent combined sewer overflow events.
Data is available from Jan 4, 2007 thru March 1, 2018.
